WBGT Heat Stress Monitor

Estimates outdoor Wet Bulb Globe Temperature from air temperature, humidity, wind, and solar conditions — the same composite index used by ACGIH and military heat-category guidance to flag exertion risk.

Field Readings

~2 m/s is a light breeze. 0 = still air.
This is about whether you are standing in the sun right now, not just whether it's sunny out — being in shade on a clear day is closer to "full shade" here.
Hot pavement feels much hotter underfoot, but at the height a globe thermometer (or your torso) sits, published field studies find surface type has only a small effect on WBGT — air mixes well above ground level. This toggle reflects that: a real but modest nudge, not a dramatic one.
ISO 7243's safe WBGT limit depends on workload: harder work lowers the threshold considerably. "Moderate" covers sustained manual work or brisk walking; "Very High" covers running or maximal-pace effort.
Per ISO 7243, "acclimatized" means regular heat exposure for at least a full week prior. Unacclimatized people have meaningfully lower safe limits.

Formula: WBGT = 0.7·Twb + 0.2·Tglobe + 0.1·Tdry (outdoor, with solar load), as defined in ISO 7243:2017. Natural wet-bulb temperature is estimated from air temperature and RH (Stull 2011). Globe temperature is estimated using the Dimiceli & Piltz (NWS Tulsa) heat-balance model, a closed-form derivative of Liljegren et al. (2008). The sky-condition picker maps to estimated solar irradiance calibrated against three published field measurements rather than theoretical peak irradiance; cross-checked against those measurements, the full WBGT pipeline lands within roughly 0.2°C. No location, date, or time data is collected to make this estimate. Reference values are taken directly from ISO 7243:2017 Annex A (WBGTref = 56.7 − 11.5·log₁₀(M) for acclimatized people, 59.9 − 14.1·log₁₀(M) for unacclimatized, M = metabolic rate in watts) — this is the standard's actual pass/fail limit; ISO itself defines no intermediate caution tiers, so the graded color bands shown here are this app's own addition for progressive feedback, not part of the standard. Treat results as estimates, not certified industrial-hygiene readings — for occupational exposure decisions, use a calibrated WBGT meter and a qualified assessment under ISO 7243 or your jurisdiction's official heat-stress guidance.
