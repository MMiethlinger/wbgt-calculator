Formula: WBGT = 0.7·Twb + 0.2·Tglobe + 0.1·Tdry (outdoor, with solar load). Natural wet-bulb temperature is estimated from air temperature and RH (Stull 2011). Globe temperature is estimated using the Dimiceli & Piltz (NWS Tulsa) heat-balance model, a closed-form derivative of Liljegren et al. (2008). The sky-condition picker maps to estimated solar irradiance calibrated against three published field measurements rather than theoretical peak irradiance; cross-checked against those measurements, the full WBGT pipeline lands within roughly 0.2°C. No location, date, or time data is collected to make this estimate. Treat results as estimates, not certified industrial-hygiene readings — for occupational exposure decisions, use a calibrated WBGT meter and your jurisdiction's official heat-stress guidance.
